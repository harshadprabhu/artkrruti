Looking after your pieces

Storing

Fold along the existing pleats and keep in a cotton bag, not plastic. Zari darkens in trapped moisture.

Cleaning

Spot clean with a dry cloth only. Never wash or soak — the gota and lace lose shape and the fabric bleeds.

Diyas & flames

Keep an open flame clear of the fabric. These are decorative pieces, not flame-treated.

Rangoli mats

Shake out and roll rather than fold. Wipe the floor dry before laying so the backing does not lift.

Questions we get often

How long does an order take?

Most pieces are made in 3 to 10 days depending on the item and how busy the festival season is. Tell us your date when you send your list and we will confirm honestly whether it is possible.

Can I get a different colour or size?

Yes — that is most of what we do. Send a photo of your mandir, thaali or door along with the measurement, and we will suggest fabric and share a price before you commit.

Do you take bulk orders?

Yes, for mandals, housing societies and wedding return gifts. Orders of 25 pieces and above are discounted. Lead time is longer, so plan ahead of the festival.

How do I pay?

We confirm the final price on WhatsApp first, then share payment details there. Nothing is charged on this website — the list you build here is an enquiry, not an order.

Do you ship outside Mumbai?

Yes, across India. Courier charges depend on weight and destination and are confirmed before dispatch.

Is the fabric real paithani?

The paithani and khan we use is woven fabric, not printed imitation. That is why colours vary slightly between batches — we will tell you if the exact shade you saw is unavailable.
